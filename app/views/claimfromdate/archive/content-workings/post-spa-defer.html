Increase your payments

You can use any State Pension we owe you to increase your regular payments for the rest of your life.

Your State Pension increases by just over 0.1% a week between 11 April 2021 and the start date you choose, as long as you choose a date after 14 June 2021.

The later your start date, the more extra State Pension you will build-up.

You will get the extra State Pension for the rest of your life but it can take more than 17 years to get back the money that you deferred.

Your State Pension date

You reached State Pension age on 5 May 2021 and may be owed some State Pension. You can split any money we owe you between:

  • extra State Pension for the rest of your life
  • a one-off arrears payment




Backdate your State Pension

You could get up to 12 months of the State Pension we owe you as a one off arrears payment.

You can get a one-off arrears payment for any State Pension you are owed from the last 12 months. The amount you get is for the period from the start date you choose to today (13 Jun 2022).

The earliest date you can backdate your start date to is 12 May 2021.

The earlier your start date, the bigger your one-off arrears payment will be.
